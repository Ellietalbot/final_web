Pruning

Regularly trim dead or yellowing leaves to promote new growth and prevent disease. For plants that bloom, deadheading flowers can also encourage more blooms.

Watering

Overwatering is a common mistake. Always check if the top 1-2 inches of soil is dry before watering. For many plants, it's better to let the soil dry out between waterings rather than keeping it constantly moist.

Humidity

Many indoor plants, like ferns and tropicals, thrive in higher humidity. You can increase humidity by misting the plant or placing it on a tray of water and pebbles, ensuring the pot doesn't sit in the water.

Fertilizing

Plants need nutrients to grow. During the growing season (typically spring and summer), feed them with a balanced, water-soluble fertilizer. Reduce fertilizing in the fall and winter when growth slows down.
